Quick Facts

What Goes Where?

Click on each category to learn more about what can and cannot be recycled.

🟦 Paper & Cardboard

✅ YES: Newspapers, magazines, office paper, cardboard boxes, paper bags

❌ NO: Pizza boxes with grease, paper towels, tissues, waxed paper, shredded paper in bags

💡 Tip: Flatten cardboard boxes to save space and remove tape or staples.

🟩 Glass

✅ YES: Bottles, jars (any color)

❌ NO: Light bulbs, mirrors, window glass, ceramics, drinking glasses

💡 Tip: Rinse containers and remove metal lids. Labels are okay to leave on.

🟨 Plastic

✅ YES: Bottles (#1, #2), containers, jugs, rigid plastics

❌ NO: Plastic bags, styrofoam, straws, plastic wrap, disposable cutlery

💡 Tip: Check the number inside the recycling symbol. When in doubt, throw it out.

🟧 Metal

✅ YES: Aluminum cans, tin cans, clean aluminum foil, metal lids

❌ NO: Aerosol cans with contents, paint cans, scrap metal, hangers

💡 Tip: Rinse food cans and crush aluminum cans to save space.

🟥 Electronics

✅ YES: Computers, phones, tablets, batteries, cables

❌ NO: Don't throw in regular recycling!

💡 Tip: Take to special e-waste collection centers. Many contain toxic materials and valuable metals.

🟫 Organic Waste

✅ YES: Fruit/vegetable scraps, coffee grounds, eggshells, yard waste

❌ NO: Meat, dairy, oils, pet waste, diseased plants

💡 Tip: Start composting at home or find a local composting program.

Practical Tips for Everyday Life

🛒 Reduce

Buy products with minimal packaging. Bring reusable bags, bottles, and containers. Choose quality over quantity.

🔄 Reuse

Repair items instead of replacing them. Donate or sell things you no longer need. Get creative with upcycling projects.

♻️ Recycle

Clean containers before recycling. Learn your local recycling rules. Keep recyclables separate from trash.

🌱 Compost

Start a compost bin for food scraps. Use compost to enrich your garden soil. Reduce methane from landfills.

💧 Conserve Water

Fix leaky faucets. Use dishwashers efficiently. Collect rainwater for plants.

⚡ Save Energy

Switch to LED bulbs. Unplug devices when not in use. Use natural light when possible.
